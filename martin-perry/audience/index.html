01· Who they are

The Lender.

Distressed & troubled asset professionals. The people who have a bad loan and need someone to take it off their hands — or better yet, save it.

Job Description
Minimize net loss on troubled commercial real-estate loans
Entry Point
Special assets team → Chief Credit Officer
Typical Spend
Significant at $5M+ deals (2–3× larger in bigger markets)
Who holds this role
  • Chief Credit Officer · Chief Lending Officer
  • Head of Special Assets / Head of Distressed Assets
  • Special Asset Officer · Workout Officer · Asset Resolution Officer
  • Any member of the special-assets group
  • Outside legal counsel (creditors'-rights & real-estate attorneys)
Where they work
  • National banks (e.g., PNC)
  • Large regional banks (United Bank, First National Bank)
  • Regional & community banks (S&T, First Commonwealth)
  • Out-of-state lenders
  • CMBS asset managers · special servicers · credit unions · FDIC
Keywords they use
  • "special asset" · "distressed asset" · "workout"
  • "asset resolution" · "special asset solutions"
  • "court-appointed receiver" · "receivership"
  • Geographic: markets others avoid (West Virginia)
Decision & timeframe
  • Day-to-day: Head of Special Assets + team
  • Escalates to Chief Credit Officer (or direct contact at community banks)
  • Attorneys heavily influence the decision — a referral gets Martin ~90% there
  • Usually engage 12–18 months after default (some wait 3 years)
02· Goals & Challenges

What keeps them up at night.

Goals — what success looks like
  • Minimize net loss to the bank; preserve collateral value
  • Resolve the problem fast — every day costs money
  • Look like a superstar to leadership & protect their career
  • Recover the bank's money through a clean disposition
  • Avoid damaging borrower relationships they personally built
Challenges — what they're fighting
  • Missed payments, defaults, broken loan covenants
  • Borrower fraud, property neglect, deferred maintenance
  • Unforeseeable events never underwritten (washed-out bridge in WV)
  • Illness/death of a key owner; heirs handing back the keys
  • Acquired banks carrying bad loans never properly underwritten
  • Conflict of interest — the borrower is often a friend
03· What we offer

Solutions & results.

Our services for this audience
  • Special-asset solutions: workout, resolution & disposition
  • Court-appointed receivership
  • Step in to secure records, assume rents, pay bills, preserve collateral
  • Rapid, prioritized troubleshooting & financial analysis
  • Front-end risk assessment on acquisitions ("fireproofing")
  • Access to investor/takeout-buyer pool for fast sales
Results they can expect
  • Erie 205-unit apartment: turned around in ~90 days, sold at a profit
  • Diffused armed-guard takeover in 15 minutes; secured ~$500K
  • Squirrel Hill: $8.5M projected loss reduced to ~$2M
  • Proven across every asset class — including two prisons (2020–21)
  • Transparent process that minimizes net loss
The critical window

Lenders typically engage 12–18 months after default — some wait 3 years. Martin's push: engage early, as a front-end advisor, not just when it's already on fire. The predicted flood of distressed inventory is coming in the back-half of 2026 through 2027–2028 — a ~6-year runway.

What they need to see first

  • Want a disinterested third party for awkward borrower conversations

02· Goals & Challenges

What drives their decisions.

Goals — what success looks like
  • Buy well — opportunistic, off-market, distressed deals at a discount ("30 cents on the dollar")
  • Build & preserve wealth; double or triple their money
  • Defer taxes (1031 exchanges) with proper planning
  • Secure favorable debt structure & interest rates

Challenges — what they're fighting
